My Video Game History

My first video game was the Pokemon series. I mainly played Pokemon Yellow and Pokemon Ruby.

However, these games soon got stolen by other kids so I would not be able to play video games. In my last year of high school, my friends in the high school IT decided to give me a copy of Starcraft they had. I played it, and it was entertaining. I often won by either amassing tons of weak units early (like a zombie raid) to win, or colonize rapidly (like rats) to gain resources to develop late-game units.

In college, I originally planned to not play any games and did not bring any games with me. However, I was put in a gaming hall way of an isolatd (compared to other dorms) gaming dorm. I ended up getting a copy of Star Wars Battlefront II as my first shooter.

Later on, my friends exposed me to various RPGs such as Fallout 3, Pokemon Omicron/Zeta, Elder Scrolls Oblivion, and Skyrim in addition to strategy games such as Europa Universalis, which motivated me to learn world history so I could crush it, Stellaris, and Kenshi.

Nowadays, I have little time to play games but since Stardew Valley does not require a lot of mods, I occasionally play the game.

However, my game library is ever increasing since I am subscribed to Humble Bundle.

My Manga/Manhwa and Anime History

While growing up, the animated TV shows I watched were some episodes of the first season of Pokemon and various Disney movies. I did not watch much TV. In college, I was introduced to various anime due to the dorm I was staying in, but none of it really stuck. In my senior year of college, however, I was looking for some sort of entertainment (My college was in suburbs, I did not want to pay expensive movie theater tickets, and I watched everything there was on Netflix at the time. ) Hearing that my friends were in an anime club, I decided to watch it.

Watching anime was entertaining. I liked reading stories while I grew up. So, instead of taking a lot of time to play video games in order to find out/create my story in a fantasy setting, I began to watch anime where the storyline was already created. Sadly, most of the links are blocked by current school servers (since I am in school right now), I am unable to provide viable links to the anime. The best anime I watched before quitting was Overlord The funniest anime was Aho (Idiot) Girl.

With the anime episodes airing slowly, I began to get frustrated with the amount of time it took to watch them. I first began to read web novels due to web novels being complete for some of the anime so that I could know the plot. For instance, I ended up reading all of That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime due to an interesting take on a reincarnation series of adventure anime.

I stopped watching anime completely when I watched One Piece since the latter episodes began to feel like I was watching 25 minutes of a staring contest that did not advance plot until five episodes later.

However, I still enjoyed viewing the imagined scenery, which led me to read manga adoptions of the anime. Currently, I have been using manga to find interesting novels and reading them.

In terms of manga (Japanese comics), manhwa (Korean comics), and manhua (Chinese comics), I dislike manhua the most since they are rarely translated well. I do not like manga as much in my favorite genre since Japanese protagonists tend to lack backbone (The protagonists forgive murderers and wonder why they keep coming to kill them, resulting in an ever increasing collateral damage.) Lately, most manhwa are translated well and have piqued my interests in some of the novels. I will list their links below (listed from what I liked the most to least).
